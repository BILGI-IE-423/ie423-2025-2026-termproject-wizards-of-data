01 — Dataset

Sephora Products & Skincare Reviews

A large-scale, multi-file dataset from Kaggle containing structured product information and unstructured customer review data, enabling both exploratory analysis and predictive machine learning.

📦 Scale

Over 8,000 products and approximately 1 million customer reviews distributed across multiple related files — one of the largest publicly available beauty product review datasets.

🧱 Structured Features

Brand info, product categories, prices, ingredients, product highlights, product ratings, skin type, skin tone, and hair color — a rich set of both product and user attributes.

📝 Unstructured Text

Detailed free-text customer reviews providing rich feedback analyzable through Natural Language Processing and Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis techniques.

02 — Preprocessing Pipeline

From Raw Reviews to
Machine-Ready Features

A multi-stage pipeline transforms raw CSV files into a structured, balanced, sentiment-enriched feature matrix ready for classification.

Data Loading & Merging

Review CSV files are combined into a unified dataframe. Corrupted rows are removed, rating columns standardized, and review + product datasets merged via product_id.

Target Variable Creation

Ratings converted to binary: 1–3 stars → Negative/Neutral and 4–5 stars → Positive.

Data Cleaning

Missing review text records cleared. Rating values converted to numeric. Invalid scores filtered. Inconsistent text values corrected.

Balanced Subsampling

A stratified sample of 5,000 reviews is selected from 797,779 reviews to reduce transformer compute cost while preserving class distribution.

ABSA (DeBERTa)

The Yangheng/deberta-v3-base-absa-v1.1 model extracts sentiment polarity, confidence scores, and sentiment intensity across three aspects: skin, hair, and general product.

Feature Engineering

Review length, vegan/cruelty-free/clean beauty indicators, active ingredient presence (hyaluronic acid, niacinamide, vitamin C, etc.), and user–product alignment scores.

Class Imbalance Management

Controlled undersampling on the majority class. Stratified train–test split. Model evaluation with Balanced Accuracy, Macro F1, ROC-AUC, and confusion matrix analysis.

03 — Research Question 1
RQ1
How accurately can product ratings be predicted using customer review sentiment analysis, product-related information, review content, and engineered product features?
Methodology

Binary Classification with ABSA + TF-IDF

Ratings between 1–3 grouped as Negative/Neutral; 4–5 as Positive. Review texts represented with TF-IDF enriched by DeBERTa ABSA aspect-level sentiment. Product characteristics (ingredients, highlights) integrated into the pipeline.

Experiments

Three ML Algorithms + Grid Search

From 797,779 original reviews, a stratified sample of 5,000 was selected. 80/20 train–test split. Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Linear SVM evaluated. GridSearchCV hyperparameter optimization applied.

Interpretation

Reviews Carry Strong Predictive Signal

Strong ROC-AUC values across all models indicate that engineered features provide high discriminatory power. DeBERTa ABSA contributes meaningful predictive information beyond traditional TF-IDF representations.

04 — Research Question 2
RQ2
Do users give higher ratings to products when their personal characteristics are aligned with product highlights?
Methodology

Rule-Based User–Product Alignment

A binary feature user_product_alignment was created by matching skin-related user characteristics to product attributes — e.g., dry skin with hyaluronic acid, oily skin with acid-based products, sensitive skin with fragrance-free formulas.

Experiments

Aligned vs. Non-Aligned Group Comparison

Dataset split into two groups: Aligned (1) and Not Aligned (0). For each group, total review count and mean rating calculated to determine whether alignment systematically produces higher ratings.

Interpretation

Personalization Drives Satisfaction

Compatibility between user characteristics and product attributes meaningfully influences satisfaction. This supports the value of personalization in cosmetic recommendation systems. Larger aligned samples would further validate this finding.

05 — Research Question 3
RQ3
How does the performance of different machine learning algorithms compare in predicting product ratings, and which model provides the highest predictive accuracy?
Methodology

Controlled Multi-Model Evaluation

Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Linear SVM evaluated against a Majority Baseline, all using identical train/test splits, preprocessing pipeline, and feature sets. GridSearchCV with cross-validation for hyperparameter optimization.

Experiments

Identical Conditions, Four Metrics

All models trained on the same balanced dataset, evaluated on the same held-out test. Performance measured by Balanced Accuracy, Macro F1-Score, ROC-AUC, and class-level precision/recall.

Interpretation

Linear SVM Best for TF-IDF Spaces

Consistent with known strengths of linear models in high-dimensional sparse feature spaces. SVM's precision–recall balance and generalization performance make it the preferred final model.

06 — Overall Discussion

Synthesis & Key Findings

🔗 Integrated Framework

By combining transformer-based sentiment analysis (DeBERTa ABSA) with classical machine learning, the project demonstrates that hybrid NLP–ML pipelines can outperform either approach alone in real-world product rating prediction tasks.

📈 Consistent Model Quality

All evaluated models significantly outperformed the Majority Baseline. While no single model dominated every metric, the collective results confirm that customer reviews and product features carry substantial predictive information about satisfaction.

🎯 Personalization Matters

The user–product alignment analysis revealed that matched products received ~1.31 points higher average ratings, highlighting the business value of personalization in recommendation systems and customer experience management.

⚖️ Class Imbalance Strategy

Controlled undersampling combined with imbalance-aware evaluation metrics (ROC-AUC, Balanced Accuracy, Macro F1) prevented dominant-class bias while enabling fair comparison across models.

Limitations

Computational Constraints

The full 797,779-review dataset could not be processed due to transformer-based ABSA compute costs. Results are based on a stratified 5,000-review sample. Cloud GPU resources could enable full-dataset analysis.

Limited Alignment Observations

The aligned group contained substantially fewer observations than the non-aligned group. Although the rating difference was substantial, larger aligned samples are needed for stronger statistical evidence.

Rule-Based Alignment

The alignment feature used predefined compatibility rules. More advanced recommendation approaches (collaborative filtering, neural embedding similarity) could better capture complex user–product relationships.

Model Trade-Offs

Positive-class precision remained lower than recall across all models, indicating some false positive predictions. Advanced sampling techniques, ensemble methods, or cost-sensitive learning could improve this further.
